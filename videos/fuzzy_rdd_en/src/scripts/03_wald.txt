So how do we estimate the treatment effect in Fuzzy RDD?

In Fuzzy RDD, only some people get treated even after crossing the cutoff. For example, 10% receive the scholarship when ineligible, and 65% receive it when eligible.

So the treatment rate rises from 10% to 65%, an increase of only 55 percentage points. If GPA rose by 0.5 points, that's the result of the additional recipients, not everyone.

So to find the effect per person who actually got treated, we divide the jump in the outcome by the jump in the treatment rate. 0.5 divided by 0.55 is about 0.91.

This is called the Wald estimator. The numerator is the change eligibility caused in the outcome; the denominator is the change eligibility caused in treatment.
