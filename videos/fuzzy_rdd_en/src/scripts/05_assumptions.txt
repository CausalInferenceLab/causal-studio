The eligibility variable Z that we've been leaning on is, more precisely, an instrumental variable. So Fuzzy RDD is fundamentally a structure that uses instrumental variables to estimate effects.

For an instrument to be valid, three assumptions must hold.

First, relevance. Becoming eligible must actually raise the probability of treatment. Statistically, the covariance between eligibility and treatment must not be zero — in terms of the Wald estimator, the denominator must not be zero. We usually check the first-stage F-statistic; if it exceeds 10, we treat the instrument as strong and consider this assumption satisfied.

Second, the exclusion restriction. Eligibility must affect the outcome only through treatment. For example, if trial assignment is the instrument, being assigned itself shouldn't directly affect health — only actually taking the drug should.

Third, monotonicity. There must be no defiers — people who refuse treatment precisely because they became eligible. This assumption is what lets us cleanly interpret the increase in treatment at the cutoff as coming entirely from compliers.

If these three assumptions hold, we can interpret the Wald estimator as the causal effect on compliers — the LATE.
