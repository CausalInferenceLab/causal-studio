In the last video, we looked at a structure where treatment is fully determined the moment you cross the cutoff.

For example, if scoring 370 or above on the CSAT — Korea's college entrance exam — gets you a scholarship, and scoring below doesn't, we called this structure Sharp RDD.

But in reality, things aren't usually this clean.

There are students who scored 371, above the cutoff, but missed the scholarship because they missed the application deadline.

And there are students who scored 369, below the cutoff, but received the scholarship anyway through a secondary review.

Crossing the cutoff doesn't mean everyone gets treated, and falling short doesn't mean no one does.

When the cutoff has a discontinuous effect on the probability of treatment without fully determining it, we call this Fuzzy RDD.

In Fuzzy RDD, crossing the cutoff causes only a partial jump in treatment probability — say, from 15% to 65%.

In this video, we'll look at how to handle Fuzzy RDD, where the jump is only partial.
