The answer is randomization.

If treatment is assigned like a coin flip, which school gets a tablet becomes completely unrelated to funding, location, or teacher quality.

The treated and control groups then become comparable on average in every way except treatment.

In notation, potential outcomes Y⁰ and Y¹ become independent of treatment assignment T.

With that single condition, both comparability requirements are automatically satisfied.

First, the expected Y⁰ for the treated group equals the expected Y⁰ for the control group.
Because of random assignment, the treated schools would have scored just as well as the control schools, had they not received the tablets.
Bias vanishes.

Second, the expected Y¹ for the treated group equals the expected Y¹ for the control group.
Both groups respond to treatment in the same way.
ATT and ATC become equal.

And so the simple mean difference equals ATE.

This is why the Randomized Controlled Trial — the RCT — is called the gold standard for causal inference.
A single randomization step secures both comparability conditions at once.
