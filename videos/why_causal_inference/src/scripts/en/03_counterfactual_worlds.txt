Now let's go beyond intuition and set up the notation we need to talk about causal effects.

First, T_i indicates whether unit i received the treatment.
It equals 1 if treated, and 0 if not.
In our example, giving a student a tablet is the treatment.

Next, Y_i is the outcome variable — what we're trying to measure.
Here, it's academic achievement.
We want to know whether the treatment affected that outcome.

But this is where a fundamental problem arises.
We can't observe the same student in both worlds — one where they got a tablet and one where they didn't.
Only one outcome exists in reality.

To handle this, we introduce the idea of potential outcomes.
Y¹_i is the potential outcome for student i if they received the tablet.
Y⁰_i is the potential outcome for that same student if they did not.

Both values are possible — but only one is ever observed.
The one that actually happened is called the factual.
The other, the road not taken, is the counterfactual.
