Intuitively, it's not hard to see why correlation isn't causation.

Say schools with tablets have higher test scores.
But what if the schools that adopted tablets were already better funded —
with more tutoring programs and better-staffed classrooms?
Those students would have done well even without the tablets.

So we can't immediately conclude that tablets improve learning.
All we can say is that there's an association between tablets and higher test scores.
