With the idea of potential outcomes, we can define the Individual Treatment Effect, or ITE.

ITE is the difference in test scores for a single school — with the tablet versus without.
That's Y¹ minus Y⁰.

It's intuitively clear, but since the counterfactual is unobservable, ITE can't be calculated directly.

So in practice, we work with the Average Treatment Effect, or ATE —
the average difference between treated and control units across the entire population.

There's also ATT: the Average Treatment effect on the Treated.
Rather than looking at everyone, ATT asks: for the units that actually received treatment,
how effective was it?
