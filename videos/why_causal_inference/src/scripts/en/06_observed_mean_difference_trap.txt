Now let's come back to reality.

The Y⁰ of treated schools and the Y¹ of control schools are never observed.
So we can't compute ITE directly — and therefore can't calculate ATE or ATT directly either.

A natural thought at this point is: why not just compare the average scores of the two groups?

Using only the observed scores in our example,
the treated schools have an average of 600 and 750 — that's 675.
The control schools average 500 and 600 — that's 550.

The difference is 125.
Based on simple averages, it looks like tablets raised scores.

But remember: the true ATE from the multiverse was negative 50.
The sign is completely reversed.

Taking observed associations at face value as causal effects can lead us badly astray.
