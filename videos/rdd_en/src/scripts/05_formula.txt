RDD estimates the treatment effect at the cutoff — known as LATE, the Local Average Treatment Effect.

This is the expected outcome just to the right of the cutoff minus the expected outcome just to the left.
If the assumptions we discussed hold, this difference is a pure causal effect.

Given data, how do we estimate this in practice?
We fit separate regression lines on each side of the cutoff, then measure the difference in their intercepts at the cutoff.

Let's walk through each parameter.

Beta zero is the expected outcome just below the cutoff — the intercept for the untreated side.

Beta two is the key parameter.
It's the jump in outcomes at the cutoff due to treatment — the treatment effect itself.

Beta one is the slope for the untreated side. Beta three captures how much the slope changes on the treated side.

The left limit at the cutoff is beta zero. The right limit is beta zero plus beta two.
Therefore, the estimated treatment effect is simply the estimate of beta two.

Keep in mind: this is a local effect, limited to units near the cutoff.
