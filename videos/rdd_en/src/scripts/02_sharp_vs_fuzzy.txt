RDD comes in two main forms: Sharp RDD and Fuzzy RDD.

Sharp RDD is the case where treatment is fully determined by crossing the cutoff.
For example, if every student who scores 370 or above is guaranteed a scholarship, that's Sharp RDD.
The graph shows treatment being perfectly determined at the 370-point cutoff.

Fuzzy RDD, on the other hand, is the case where the cutoff affects the probability of treatment, but doesn't fully determine it.
For example, if a student can score above 370 but still not apply for the scholarship, or fail a secondary review, that's Fuzzy RDD.
The treatment probability jumps at the cutoff, but it doesn't go from 0 to 1 completely — as you can see in the graph.
